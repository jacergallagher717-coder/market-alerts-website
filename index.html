Options Basics

Call Options: Contract giving you the RIGHT (not obligation) to BUY stock at a specific strike price. You profit when the stock goes UP above your strike.

Put Options: Contract giving you the RIGHT to SELL stock at strike price. You profit when stock goes DOWN below your strike.

Strike Price: The agreed price to buy/sell. Choose based on your conviction - closer strikes cost more but are more likely to profit.

Expiration Date: When the option expires. More time = higher premium = more expensive.

Premium: The price you pay for the option. This is your maximum risk - options can go to $0.

Understanding The Greeks

Delta (Œî): How much the option price moves for every $1 stock move. Delta 0.30-0.40 means balanced risk/reward. Higher delta = more stock-like behavior.

Theta (Œò): Time decay. How much value your option loses each day. Options lose value faster as expiration approaches, especially in the final 30 days.

Vega (ŒΩ): Sensitivity to volatility changes. Big news = volatility spike = option premiums increase. Great for buyers, bad for sellers.

Gamma (Œì): Rate of delta change. Higher for at-the-money options. Accelerates your gains OR losses as stock moves.

Smart Position Sizing

The 1-2% Rule: Never risk more than 1-2% of your total account on any single trade. This protects you from catastrophic losses and keeps you in the game.

Options Sizing: Start with 0.5-1% of account due to higher volatility and leverage. Options can expire worthless, so size accordingly.

Shares Sizing: Can allocate 1-3% with proper stop losses. Less risky than options but still requires discipline.

Diversification: Don't put all capital in one sector. Spread across uncorrelated positions to reduce portfolio volatility.

Risk Management Rules

Always Use Stop Losses: Pre-determine your exit if the trade goes against you. Typically 3-5% for shares, 50% loss for options premiums.

Take Profits: Greed kills accounts. When you hit your target (20-50% for options, 5-10% for shares), take profits. You can always re-enter.

Thesis Invalidation: If the reason you entered changes (news reverses, data contradicts), exit immediately regardless of profit/loss.

Never Average Down: Don't add to losing positions hoping they recover. Cut losers fast, let winners run with trailing stops.
